Alan Turing

The man who solved the German naval Enigma

Quote from the movie The Imitation Game.

Here is a timeline of Alan Turing's life:

  • June 23, 1912 - Born in London, England.
  • 1918 - Starts school at six years old and shows talent.
  • February 1930 - Best friend Christopher dies.
  • 1931-1934 - Studies at King's College, Cambrige.
  • June 1938 - Obtains Ph.D. from Princeton in Mathematic Logic.
  • September 1938 - Starts working part-time with the British codebreaking organization, GC&CS.
  • Late 1939 - He and Dilly Knox start working on decrypting Enigma code messages.
  • December 1939 - He breaks the Enigma code using a machine (Turing Machine) he created.
  • 1945-1947 - Worked on the design of the ACE (Automatic Computing Engine) in Hampton, London.
  • January 1952 - Admits having homosexual relations (a crime) to police while they investigated a burglary at his home.
  • June 7, 1954 - Dies of cyanide poisoning.
